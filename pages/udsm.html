UNIVERSITY DISTRICT STREET MEDICINE

PROVIDING QUALITY CARE FOR INDIVIDUALS EXPERIENCING HOMELESSNESS

The student-run, interdisciplinary organization was searching for a team to replace their WIX website with a completely new front-end and back-end redesign. After being recommended by a friend to be a part of the team, I was given the opportunity to take on the role as the Web Designer for the University District Street Medicine redesign project.

THE PROCESS

The Co-Director of Operations and I worked together in a rapid prototyping approach to quickly mock up the future state of the website through an iterative three-step process of prototyping, reviewing, and refining. Each cycle led to a better design that would help reduce the need for major design changes during development.

MY ROLE

As the Web Designer, I was responsible for creating a brand new UDSM website experience by adopting principles of minimal design. The steps I took to deliever a high-fidelty prototype included sketching, wireframing, and user testing from September 2016 - January 2017.

THE REDESIGN PROCESS

RESTRUCTURING THE FRAMEWORK

Before focusing on any visual elements, I spent a great amount of time making sense of how content from the previous UDSM website can be better organized from page to page. Using a card sorting technique, I was able to create a framework that combined related information into categories that would make sense for first-time visitors. Mapping out where all the content lived first, made it easier to determine how the information can be linked all together and accessed.

FINDING INSPIRATION

The first step taken into the visual design began with searching for inspiration online that demonstrated concepts of minimalism. My findings led me to create a mood board that I believed would appropriately represent the feel and aesthetic that the UDSM was in need of.

WIREFRAMING

Prototyping in a medium-fidelity best illustrated if website goals were met and if the user experience was optimal. This collaborative process became the most time-consuming part of the redesign process as we progressively revised the prototype until we found a design that best fit the UDSM vision.

PROTOTYPE TESTING

I worked closely with the developers to create a high-fidelity working prototype that would be used for usability testing. Results told us there was too much white space between elements, making each page less fluid to follow and read. Users were able to easily complete scenario tasks that ran parallel with the ideal user flow. Together, the developers and I were able to quickly make adjustments to the prototype after each test session.
