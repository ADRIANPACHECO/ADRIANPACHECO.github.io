University District Street Medicine: Providing quality care for individuals experiencing homelessness.

Fall 2016 - Winter 2017

The student-run, interdisciplinary organization was searching for a team to replace their WIX website with a completely new front-end and back-end redesign. After being recommended by a friend to be a part of the team, I was given the opportunity to take on the role as the Web Designer for the UDSM redesign project. allowed me to work in close collaboration with the Director and the Co-Director of Operations at UDSM allowed me to produce I was able to design a high-fidelty prototype as a final deliverable for the development team.


CLIENT: University District Street Medicine
FRONT-END DEVELOPER: Morya Breland
BACK-END DEVELOPER: Jonathan Li

The project details.

THE PROCESS

The Co-Director of Operations and I worked together in a rapid prototyping approach to quickly mock up the future state of the website through an iterative three-step process of prototyping, reviewing, and refining. Each cycle led to a better design that would help reduce the need for major design changes during development.

MY ROLE

As the Web Designer, I was responsible for creating a brand new UDSM website experience by adopting principles of minimal design. The steps I took to deliever a high-fidelty prototype included sketching, wireframing, and user testing.

The redesign process.

RESTRUCTURING THE FRAMEWORK

Before focusing on any visual elements, I spent a great amount of time making sense of how content from the previous UDSM website can be better organized from page to page. Using a card sorting technique, I was able to create a framework that combined related information into categories that would make sense for first-time visitors. Mapping out where all the content lived first, made it easier to determine how the information can be linked all together and accessed.


FINDING INSPIRATION

The first step taken into the visual design began with searching for inspiration online that demonstrated concepts of minimalism. My findings led me to create a mood board that I believed would appropriately represent the feel and aesthetic that the UDSM was in need of.

BRAINSTORMING AND IDEATION

Once we were able to establish the website's Information Architecture, I then began sketching on paper of what the website can potentially look like with UI elements applied from the mood board. Working in this type of fidelity gave me the opportunity to rapidly brainstorm ideas without having any major design commitments.

WIREFRAMING

Prototyping in a medium-fidelity best illustrated whether website goals were met and whether the user experience was optimal. This collaborative process became the most time-consuming part of the redesign process as we progressively revised the prototype until we found a design that best fit the UDSM vision.

PROTOTYPE TESTING

I worked closely with the developers to create a high-fidelity working prototype that would be used for usability testing. Results told us there was too much white space between elements, making each page less fluid to follow and read. Users were able to easily complete scenario tasks that ran parallel with the ideal user flow. Together, the developers and I were able to quickly make adjustments to the prototype hoping to gain better results after each test session.
