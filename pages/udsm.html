UNIVERSITY DISTRICT STREET MEDICINE WEB DESIGN

Client | Unversity District Street Medicine
Developers | Jonathan Li, Morya Breland
Role | Web Designer
Year | 2017


The University District Street Medicine (UDSM) was searching for students to replace
their WIX website with a completely new front-end and back-end redesign.
As the designer for the project, I worked in close collaboration with the Director and
the Co-Director of Operations at UDSM in order to produce a working Adobe XD prototype as a final
deliverable for the development team.

NEW YEAR, NEW LOOK.

The newly designed University District Street Medicine (UDSM) website is created with the essence of minimalism at its core.
The interface includes a simple, elegant, and intuitive design for users interested in learning about the non-profit
organization's dedication to improving health equity among the University District homeless population.

Home.
It's where the heart is.


The key to the Home page is to act as the table of contents of a book. It is to clearly state what the entire website is, what it includes, what you can do, and why should you be there.

In comparison to the early days of the Web, people are more likely directed into a page deep within a website by clicking on links from social networks, emails, and blogs. When this happens, people often get themselves deeper into a website without ever knowing how each page ties together. This makes the Home page an area of reliance when lost.

Less reading. More scanning.


Let's face it, we do not read pages anymore, we scan them. We look for keywords, bullet points, clickable elements, and compelling images. The newly designed UDSM website presents the most important information by using less of it.

INSPIRATION.

SIMPLE TOOLS FOR SIMPLE IDEAS.

Using Adobe Experience Design turned ideas into prototypes in the most effective way possible.
It made concept refinement and sharing easy to deliver a high fidelity prototype.

Project challenge.


Surprisingly, the challenges I faced did not come from ideation or concept creation, but rather in transferring my works over to the development team. Fortunately, I was able to communicate the logistical information and important details of the web design, but not in a way that I believe a professional would do so. The important takeaway was that I made it work with the tools I had.
